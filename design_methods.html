Overview on Design Methods

There are different approaches to design, and they all focus on different parts of the process. Some are about generating ideas, others about testing, and some are more about questioning things.

Brainstorming is mainly about creating many ideas quickly without judging them. Co-design involves users directly in the process. Critical design is less about solving problems and more about questioning ideas and provoking thoughts.

Rapid prototyping and iterative design are closely connected, because both focus on building, testing and improving ideas. Research through design is about learning by making and experimenting.

While these methods are different, they are often used together. For example, you might start with brainstorming, then build a prototype, test it and iterate on it.

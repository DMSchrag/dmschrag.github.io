Home Improvements

I used some 12mm plywood a jigsaw (ugh they just can't cut in a straight line, we got a circular saw now but damn why did anyone even make jigsaws) I obviously wanted my cat to have a 10/10 experience while doing her business and thought about including a small window! Hexapod

A hexapod is a 6 legged robot with a total of 18 joints, the first time I ever saw one I was absulutly infuriated, like damn man those things rock, we dont talk about efficency here mkay. Yeah so I head dived straight into designing my own because everything else would have been lame, design is terrible and bad but I learnt a lot from the first version.

Iteration Process

After a lot of adjustments I printed it put a Pi Zero on it an IMU some buck converters on the bottom and 2 16 Channel PWM Servo Driver board, I calculated the Inverse Kinematics and wrote the code, meaning I can tell each leg how to move from its origin point. I came to a halt when I got into my final year of my bachelor and everything was just A LOT. Also I programmed everything in VIM, because the PI Zero is quite weak to program in its desktop environment and I didnt explore other options, I learnt a lot about using VIM but now I just like to do it over SSH in visual studio code. Goodbye VIM you wont be missed.

RC Crawler

Mini Wing Prototype

Yeah so I like flying wings, I find them beautifull, they can utilise their whole area to generate lift. This is a small one build with some left over styro. The airfoil is a HS522 speed profile. I had differential thurst on it with 2 BLDC motors and as flight controller some pixhawk or something similiar and some lightweight servos left and right. It never flew, it always rolled to the right side. To be fair there are no winglets for stabilisation. In the future I would like to make one out of balsa and laser cut and laminate it.

Robot Car

Yeah, so what you can see here or well not because the angle is bad. Is a robot car it got a self designed PCB with a DCDC converter on it and a buck. There is a TOF on the car a gyro a small display and as brain an STM32 nucleo board. It was able to be controlled from my mobile phone via bluetooth and had obstacle avoidance. First time designing and soldering a PCB. Next time I will buy the PCB pre assembled. Learnt a lot of desoldering on that one ugh.

Hexapod

A hexapod is a 6 legged robot with a total of 18 joints, the first time I ever saw one I was absulutly infuriated, like damn man those things rock, we dont talk about efficency here mkay. Yeah so I head dived straight into designing my own because everything else would have been lame, design is terrible and bad but I learnt a lot from the first version.

Iteration Process

After a lot of adjustments I printed it put a Pi Zero on it an IMU some buck converters on the bottom and 2 16 Channel PWM Servo Driver board, I calculated the Inverse Kinematics and wrote the code, meaning I can tell each leg how to move from its origin point. I came to a halt when I got into my final year of my bachelor and everything was just A LOT. Also I programmed everything in VIM, because the PI Zero is quite weak to program in its desktop environment and I didnt explore other options, I learnt a lot about using VIM but now I just like to do it over SSH in visual studio code. Goodbye VIM you wont be missed.

Flying Wing

So during my final term and parallel to my bachelor thesis I fell in love with flying wings, and we had parallel flying robotics, so obviously I had to build one!! I started with going to the lecturer and asking him for some guidance, which he gladly provided, I had a website fro references some freeware software to design the wing, and on I went! The first part was the design, I used frank ranis Nurflugel LAZ software, old but still good, I had good stability values and a near elliptic lift curve. Also my lecturer took about hours of his time to help my tweak the parameters to make it actually fly (Thanks to Ulrich, geiler typ isch das) We settled for a lift producing airfoil in the middle MH30 and a speed profile on the sides HS522, Wingspan 140cm, Sweep 28deg, Twist -5, Stability margin was about 9.4% and an aspect ratio of 6.74, the wing is made of EPP and was cut with a self made hot foam wire cutter with selfmade wooden profiles on the sides. It was okaysh smove if you asked Ulrich it was garbade, but it would fly he said. We had a Matek H743 as flight controller, and a really cheap BLDC motor, coudlnt even get a data sheet, but at least we had a 40A ESC, so nothing burnt down. He was able to perform a short distance flight but had a lot of resonance and flapping!

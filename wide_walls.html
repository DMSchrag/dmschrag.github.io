Wide Walls in Tinkering

Looking at materials that allow many different outcomes. (Wide Walls)

What are Wide Walls?

Wide walls basically means that you can do many different things with the same material. So instead of having one fixed result, there are many possible directions you can go in.

I think this is important because not everyone wants to build the same thing. With wide walls, people can follow their own ideas and still use the same tools or materials.

Cardboard Construction

Cardboard is a really good example because it is simple but very flexible. You can build structures, machines or just creative objects, even though I have never personally build a lot with cardboard..

LEGO

LEGO is probably the most obvious example, just as a side remark, that one should not support LEGO anymore, because of their unethical practices, but it is still a good example of wide walls. But I prefer other manufacturers like Bluebrixx!! Everyone uses the same bricks, but the results are always completely different.

This makes it very open and not limited to one solution, which is exactly what wide walls are about.

Chain Reactions

Chain reaction projects are also a good example because there is no single correct setup. You can connect elements in many different ways.

These kinds of projects are very open and allow a lot of experimentation, I personally find them neat, but its not my cup of tea.

Reflection

I think wide walls are really important in tinkering because they make the process more creative, I just don't like the term. If there is only one correct solution, it feels more like following instructions than actually building something.

The examples show that simple materials like cardboard or LEGO can actually be more powerful than complex systems, because they give more freedom, since they are the basic fundamentals.
